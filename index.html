Do-Gon

I'm a graduate research assistant at Columbia University, working in the DitecT Lab and ROAM Lab. My research focuses on applying sensing technologies to robotic systems, including tactile sensing, acoustic sensing, and robotic manipulation.

My projects include developing methods for real-time acoustic sensing in robotics, contributing to the Vibecheck project, and designing safer control systems for robotic rehabilitation devices such as the C-ALEX system. My goal is to enhance the safety, functionality, and robustness of robotic systems through cutting-edge research.

Research

My research focuses on acoustic sensing, tactile sensing, and robotic manipulation. I'm particularly interested in developing systems that can understand and respond to the physical world using advanced sensing technologies.

Recent Projects

  • Vibecheck: Explored active acoustic sensing for robotic manipulation, including analyzing the resonance of different materials.
  • Rehabilitation Robotics: Improved control systems for cable-driven devices (e.g., C-ALEX) by integrating piezoelectric sensors for real-time wire tension measurement.
  • DeepRacer Project: Developed autonomous vehicle algorithms for computer vision and SLAM.

Teaching

I serve as a teaching assistant for the Intro to Robotics course (MECE4602) at Columbia University, where I create assignments, host office hours, and assist students in understanding robotics concepts such as kinematics, Jacobian, and singularities.
