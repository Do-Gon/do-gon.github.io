Do-Gon Kim

I'm currently a MS student @ Columbia, working in the ROAM Lab as a graduate research assistant. My research focuses on robotics, particularly in areas such as tactile sensing, object classification through signal processing and machine learning, and vision-based robotic control systems.

Before joining Columbia, I received my Bachelors degree in Mechanical Engineering @ NYU, where I worked in the Control/Robotics Research Lab (CRRL) on the autonomous navigation using SLAM algorithms and sensor integration.

Research

My research focuses on acoustic sensing, tactile sensing, and robotic manipulation. I'm particularly interested in developing systems that can understand and respond to the physical world using advanced sensing technologies.

Recent Projects

  • Vibecheck: Explored active acoustic sensing for robotic manipulation, including material classification, Internal structure classification, object state classification, grasp point estimation, contact type estimation.
  • DeepRacer Project: Developed a simulation framework using ROS2 and PyBullet in AWS DeepRacer, enabling ML model testing without physical hardware and fostering a dynamic environment for autonomous vehicle research.
